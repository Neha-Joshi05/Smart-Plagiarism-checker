Climate change means long term changes in temperatures and weather conditions.
While some of these changes occur naturally since the 1800s human activities
have been the primary cause of climate change mainly through the burning of
fossil fuels such as coal petroleum and natural gas. The combustion of fossil
fuels produces greenhouse gas emissions that function like a blanket around
Earth trapping solar heat and increasing global temperatures. Carbon dioxide
and methane are the primary greenhouse gases responsible for climate change.
These emissions originate from activities like driving vehicles burning coal
for heating and deforestation which releases stored carbon. Agriculture and
fossil fuel extraction are also significant contributors to methane emissions.

Solar energy is one of the most abundant renewable energy sources available.
It can be harnessed through photovoltaic panels or solar thermal systems.
Wind energy is another important renewable source that uses turbines to
convert kinetic energy from wind into electrical power for homes and businesses.
